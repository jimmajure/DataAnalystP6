Overview (click to collapse)

Explore air temperature observation data collected from 83 weather buoys located in the equitorial Pacific Ocean. The data are part of NOAA's NDBC (National Data Buoy Center). The data were collected in the period from 1979 thru 2015.

The Map View shows the locations of the buoys. Click on a buoy marker to select the it and highlight its temperature data in the Graph View.

The Graph View displays the median temperature values for all stations over the period from 1979 thru 2015. Hover the mouse over a line to see which station it is from and highlight it on the map.

You can choose to view the Annual median value or the median for a specific month of the year.

There are several patterns/trends that can be discovered in the data.

  • Temperatures in the eastern part of the area are generally lower than those in the west. Hover over lines in the Graph View to investigate.
  • The data is more sparse in the early years and more complete in later years.
  • In 1997, temperatures rise noticeably due to the El Nino weather patter that year.
  • Temperatures vary less during the early spring months (February, March, April), than in other months.
  • Temperatures generally rise over the course of time, although there is considerable variability.
